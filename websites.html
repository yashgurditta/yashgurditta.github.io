Where it all Started

I first learned how to develop websites when I was 15. My goal was to build a website for my father's business using a service like Squarespace or Weebly. I quickly learned that what I wanted to acheive could only realistically be done by custom coding a site from scratch. After watching several hours of YouTube videos, reading dozens of Stack Overflow questions, and completing the CodeAcademy HTML/CSS course, I began making strides on my site. Since then I've built several sites for fun and for clients. My sophomore year of college at Tufts, I took a course called COMP 20: Web Programming with Ming Chow which opened my eyes to a ton of new backend features I hadn't experimented with previously. Though I learned a lot and enjoy back end development, I still find myself going back to front end design and development. More recently, I've become very interested in developing E-Commerce websites optimized for generating revenue.
